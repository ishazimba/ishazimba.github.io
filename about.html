STUDENT MENTAL WELLBEING APPLICATION

Student Mental Wellbeing Application (SMWA)

This project focuses on the design and development of a Student Mental Well-Being Application, with the aim of addressing the critical issue of mental well-being among university students. Extensive research has shown that mental well-being plays a significant role in an individual's emotional, psychological, and social state, influencing their thoughts, feelings, and reactions to various situations. Given the high prevalence of mental well-being disorders among university students, with estimates ranging from 12% to 46% affected annually (Harrer et al., 2019), this project seeks to understand the specific needs, challenges, experiences, and behaviors of these students. To achieve this, a comprehensive user research approach was employed, utilizing an online survey targeting university students. The obtained research findings were carefully evaluated and utilized in the development of key artifacts such as the empathy map and storyboard. These artifacts, along with the research insights, were instrumental in identifying both the functional and non-functional requirements of the SMWA. The ultimate goal was to create a high fidelity prototype of an Student Mental Well-being Application that addresses the daily needs and aspirations of its users, empowering them to effectively manage their mental well-being. Through a professional and systematic approach, this project endeavors to contribute to the well-being and success of university students by providing them with a user-centric and purposeful digital solution.

Empathy Map

The empathy map provides a visual representation of the users' perspectives, allowing for a deeper exploration of their goals, challenges, and motivations related to mental well-being. By synthesizing the survey data, distinct personas were formed to represent different user segments. Each persona within the empathy map encapsulates specific user characteristics and experiences, shedding light on their unique requirements. This helps guide the design process and ensures that the SMWA addresses the users' specific needs effectively. The empathy map serves as a valuable reference throughout the design journey, enabling the team to maintain a user-centered approach. It facilitates the identification of opportunities to enhance the user experience, tailor features to address their needs, and create an application that fosters a supportive environment for mental well-being. In summary, the empathy map derived from the survey results provides a concise and professional tool to understand the users' perspectives, allowing for the development of the SMWA in a manner that is aligned with their requirements and aspirations.

Story Board

The storyboard created for the SMWA (Student Mental Well-Being Application) portrays the user's narrative when encountering challenges and difficulties during their student life. It visually depicts the journey of a user facing various problems and pains that impact their mental well-being. The storyboard highlights the pivotal moments that prompt the user to seek a solution and download the SMWA for support. By presenting this user story, the storyboard helps to emphasize the relevance and significance of the application in addressing the specific needs and concerns of students.
